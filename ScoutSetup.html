Step 1 of 4: Introduction to WARS Scouting Flow

You're about to tell WARS information about who you have available to scout other teams. WARS will intelligently generate a list of high-priority matches you should focus on.

WARS Suggested Matches are:

  • Easy. WARS will tell you what matches to focus, but if you can't make one, there will plenty of other valuable matches you can watch.
  • Optimized. WARS optimizes match suggestion using a number of factors, including who's available to scout when and what teams you're interested in.

Before you start, make sure:

  • your event has team and match data available on RobotEvents. WARS works best when the data updates in real-time.
  • you know who your scouts will be, their email addresses, and the teams they are competing with (if any) at this tournament. WARS will email scouts a list of matches they may be able to watch.
  • you have sufficient time to complete this process. At this time, WARS doesn't save your data after each step. If you close this dialog, you'll lose everything you entered.
Step 2 of 4: Scout Information

Type or paste the names, email addresses, and the teams (that a scout is actively playing on) of your scouts below. Separate names and email addresses with a comma and a space (, ). Put new names on a new line (press enter after each email address). For example: Rob Ott, rob.ott@example.com, 1234R

Step 3 of 4: High-Interest Teams

In the box below, enter the team numbers of the teams you're interested in. Make sure to press Enter or Return after each team number.
